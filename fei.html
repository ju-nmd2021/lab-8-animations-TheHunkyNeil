Introduction to Pop

Pop music is a kind of music style, which refers to music created with the main purpose of catering to the tastes of a broad audience. It is commercial music entertainment and all related "industrial" phenomena. Its marketability is primary, and its artistry is secondary. Popular music originated in the United States at the end of the 19th century and the beginning of the 20th century. From the perspective of the music system, popular music is music developed on the basis of American popular music structures such as Ding Bang Alley music, blues, jazz, rock music, and Thor music. Its styles are diverse and rich in forms, which can generally refer to urban mass music born after the 20th century, such as Jazz, Rock, Soul, Blues, Reggae, Rap, Hip-Hop, Disco, and New Age.

Folk


Ballads , popular folk songs, rich in national color, called folk songs or folk songs. Folk songs have a long history, so their authors are mostly unknown. The folk songs are rich in content, including religious, romantic, war, and work, as well as drinking, dancing, music, and ceremonies. Both express the feelings and customs of a nation, so each has its own unique scale and emotional style. For example, the prosperity of French folk songs, the enthusiasm of Italian folk songs, the simplicity of British folk songs, the grief and indignation of Japanese folk songs, the unruly and unruly Spanish folk songs, and the lingering enthusiasm of Chinese folk songs all show a strong national temperament and color. Taiwanese folk songs are greatly influenced by Japanese rock and folk songs.
